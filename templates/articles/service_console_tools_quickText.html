In the fast-paced world of customer service, every second counts. Quick Text is a powerful Salesforce Service Console productivity tool designed to save your agents time and ensure consistent communication.

What is Quick Text?

Quick Text allows users to predefined messages—like greetings, common notes, standard phrases, or answers to frequently asked questions. instead of typing the same response fifty times a day, users can insert these messages into emails, chats, and other channels with a simple button click or keyboard shortcut.

Why use Quick Text?

  • Standardization: Ensure every agent uses the comprehensive, approved branding and language.
  • Efficiency: The ultimate replacement for manual copy/pasting from external documents.

Designing Your Strategy

Before you toggle the switch in Setup, it is crucial to plan your implementation. Quick Text works best when it is organized. Consider these questions:

  • Channel Strategy: Who should be able to use Quick Text? Does this apply to Email, Chat, messaging, or internal notes?
  • Creation Rights: Who should be able to create messages? Should every agent make their own, or should it be restricted to managers?
  • Sharing Logic: Who needs access to which messages? Do you want unique sets of messages for different business units (e.g., Sales vs. Support)?
  • Use Cases: In which specific scenarios does your team struggle with repetitive typing?

How to Enable & Configure

Setting up Quick Text is straightforward:

  1. Go to Setup.
  2. In the Quick Find box, type "Quick Text Settings".
  3. Enable the feature.

Understanding Permissions

Simply enabling the feature doesn't always grant full access. Here is how the permissions break down:

  • Read Access (Default): Most standard user profiles have default Read permission. This means they can view and insert existing Quick Text messages without additional setup.
  • Create/Edit Access: To allow users (like Team Leads or Senior Agents) to create their own messages, you must update their User Profile or assign a Permission Set with "Create" and "Edit" permissions on the Quick Text object.

Real-World Scenarios

Here are two common examples of how Quick Text transforms workflows.

Scenario 1: Standardizing Customer Support

The Problem: Service reps frequently close cases where customers need instructions on how to open a specific product package. Typing this out manually leads to errors.

The Solution: Create a Quick Text record with the following body:

"Follow these initial steps to open the packaging:
1. Open the lock from the 4 corners.
2. Carefully remove the protective wrapper."

Scenario 2: Internal Manager Tasks

The Problem: Managers often need to assign similar tasks to multiple service reps regarding customer satisfaction checks.

The Solution: A Manager (with Create permissions) creates a standard task description:

"Please check on the last customer’s feedback and update the record."
